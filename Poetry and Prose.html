Ebbing Away

Jacqueline Bonilla, 2016

Posted on January 15, 2016

Is it comfort that I want,
or an ear to feel my breath?

Shall we dance to the music 
Of the stars
Until our atoms join them?

Beauty fades and body fails
As I search for immortality.

Perfect conditions for new star to shine
Will surround my entity.

Shadow Dust

Jacqueline Bonilla, 2015

Posted on January 8, 2016

Negatives of bones and iron frames
Warmth of terrors calling beads of sweat.
Breath through teeth,
And tightening of chest.

Autumn comes nevertheless
Dancing with the soggy death.
But what of it?

Prescriptions past due
A rocking chair through
Of rocking
      A shadow of dust.

Glances

Jacqueline Bonilla, 2015

Posted on January 6, 2016

Flowers die under my gaze
Is it suicide or murder?
I hold their power in my breath.

Poppy Rising

Jacqueline Bonilla, 2015

Posted on January 5, 2016

Join me in this game
That strains against the bounds
Which fix our bodies

Fingers in a web
Provide an evanescent hush
To turpid darkness

Midmorning glow of 
Exhausting lamps and rich sun
On weary faces.

Musky smell of rain
(to waft away transgressions)

It is not in vain.

No longer in vain.
